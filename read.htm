The article

As many as half of all stars in the universe lie in the vast gulfs of space between galaxies, an unexpected discovery made in a new study using NASA rockets. These stars could help solve mysteries regarding missing light and particles that theory had suggested should exist, scientists say. In the study, astronomers investigated the extragalactic background light, the sum of all light emitted by stars in the universe throughout history.
